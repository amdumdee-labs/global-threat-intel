## What This Is

A daily-updated archive of cybersecurity threat intelligence, analyzed through a business lens and written for the people who make the decisions — not the people who run the tools.

This is **original analysis**, not aggregation. Each report takes the day's real incidents and translates them into what they mean for revenue, liability, governance, and competitive position. Every report cites its sources.

- **Business-translated** — threats explained in board language, not CVE scores
- **Daily** — fresh analysis tracking how the landscape actually moves
- **Original** — synthesis and interpretation, not a news feed
- **Cited** — every report traces back to its sources, transparently

## How to Read a Report

Each report is a structured YAML file. Start with the `executive_summary` for the board-ready version, then go deeper.

| Section | What it gives you |
|---|---|
| `executive_summary` | The board-ready briefing in plain English |
| `what_happened` | The incident in business terms |
| `why_it_matters` | Financial, regulatory, competitive, operational impact |
| `strategic_response` | Executive decisions required — not IT task lists |
| `analysis_and_insights` | The uncomfortable truths and strategic lessons |
| `forward_outlook` | Where this is headed over 6–12 months |
| `source_articles` | Every source cited, with its contribution noted |

Prefer it rendered? Read any report at **[amdumdee.com](https://amdumdee.com)**.

## Important Notes

This repository provides analysis and interpretation of publicly available threat intelligence, business-impact assessments based on available information, and strategic framing based on industry best practice.

It does **not** provide legal advice, financial advice, specific security implementations, guarantees of accuracy, or incident-response services. Business-impact estimates are approximations. Every organization's risk profile is unique — always validate intelligence with your own security team.
